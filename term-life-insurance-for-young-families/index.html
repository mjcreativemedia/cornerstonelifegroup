Built for the years that matter most

Term life is often considered by parents ages 25 to 45 because it can provide larger coverage amounts for a defined period. The goal is to help loved ones continue life financially if a parent or income earner dies unexpectedly.

How much coverage should you review?

A practical review may include income replacement, remaining mortgage balance, childcare, education goals, debts, and final expenses. The right amount depends on your family, budget, health, and state availability.

Term life questions for parents

Is term life only for families with children?

No, but children create one of the strongest reasons to review income replacement. Homeowners, spouses, and business owners may also use term life.

Can term life help with mortgage protection?

Yes. Many families use term life as part of a mortgage protection strategy because the benefit can give loved ones flexible money during a difficult time.
